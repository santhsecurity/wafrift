# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as
contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in this
project and its community a harassment-free experience for everyone,
regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex
characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience,
education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance,
race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behaviour that contributes to creating a positive
environment:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism.
- Focusing on what is best for the community.
- Showing empathy towards other community members.

Examples of unacceptable behaviour:

- The use of sexualised language or imagery and unwelcome sexual
  attention or advances.
- Trolling, insulting/derogatory comments, and personal or political
  attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information, such as a physical or
  electronic address, without explicit permission.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in
  a professional setting.

## Lawful Use & Repository Responsibility

This project ships dual-use security tooling. In addition to the
general standards above, contributors and users agree to:

1. **Use the tool only against authorised targets.** Running wafrift
   against a system you do not own and have not been explicitly
   authorised to test — by bug-bounty scope, signed penetration-test
   agreement, CTF competition rules, or a lab you operate — is
   misuse, regardless of intent. The full lawful-use clause is in
   [`SECURITY.md`](./SECURITY.md#lawful-use--repository-responsibility).
2. **Do not solicit help with unauthorised testing.** Issues, pull
   requests, discussions, and direct messages that ask maintainers
   or other contributors to assist with attacks on systems the
   requester is not authorised to test will be closed without further
   engagement and may be reported to the affected organisation's
   abuse channel.
3. **Do not contribute exploit code that targets a specific live
   third party.** Generic evasion techniques, CVE-replay fixtures
   pinned to public PoC code, and corpus entries derived from
   published advisories are in scope. Operational tooling crafted
   for a single named live victim is not.
4. **Responsibility for traffic stays with the operator.** The
   maintainers do not consent to, instruct, or take responsibility
   for any traffic generated by your use of the tool. Liability for
   damages or legal exposure transfers entirely to the operator at
   the moment they invoke the binary.

Contributors who demonstrate a pattern of unlawful use, or who
attempt to use the project's infrastructure (issues, discussions,
discord, email) to coordinate attacks on unauthorised targets, will
have their access to project channels revoked and credible reports
may be forwarded to the affected organisation.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behaviour —
including violations of the Lawful Use clause above — may be reported
by contacting the project team at
**security@santh.dev**. All complaints will be reviewed and
investigated and will result in a response that is deemed necessary
and appropriate to the circumstances. The project team is obligated to
maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter of an incident.
